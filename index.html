Dear Jewel,

my darling, if you’re reading this, it means you’ve looked through to the deepest part of what I build for you. I wanted to do something for you for the longest while. I wanted to do something for you to show my appreciation. I wanted to buy you things, create something physically, but what I landed on is this. Something that I hope you can come back to whenever you need, whenever you want to feel close to me, whenever you want to feel loved by me. I hope this is something that can be a source of comfort and joy for you, just like how you are for me.

I’m sorry that I’ve disappointed you at times. I think in trying to love you well, I’ve also had moments where I didn’t show up in the ways you needed me to. Recently, I’ve given you more space because I’ve reflected and realized there are parts of the role you sometimes need from others that I struggle with naturally. Meaning, I’m not always as outgoing or as “chalant” as you might want me to be, and I understand how that can feel like a gap between us. I’ve noticed that in those moments, you find those things elsewhere, and while I can’t control that, I don’t want to respond with insecurity or anger. I’d rather be honest about who I am, and keep working on becoming better in the ways I can grow. What matters to me most is that we understand each other clearly, not that I try to be someone I’m not.

I love you. I know I’m not perfect. I can be a burden, a nuisance, a pushover, and honestly a forgetful loser more often than I’d like to admit. But I want you to know I am trying. Really trying. To grow, to be better, to love you in a way that feels safe and real and steady. You mean the world to me and I think about our wedding a lot. About us building something real together, stepping into a new chapter of life where it’s not just you and me in singuklar moments, but you and me in everything we choose to build going forward. And I want that future with you.

At the same time, I need our love to be something that feels honest on both sides. Something where we choose each other fully, not halfway. I’m here because I want to be. Because I choose you. Every time. Even if you think I am oblivious to your feelings, even if you think I am not paying attention, even if you think I am not trying, I want you to know that I am. I am here.

And I hope you choose me too.

Always.

You’ve been one of the most important parts of my life, more than I probably say out loud.

I’m proud of you. I care about you. And I’m here, even in the quiet moments.

Your forgetful, loving, and always trying boyfriend,


Chancellor Duncan
